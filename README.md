# Business Process Mapping & Optimization Case Study

## Project Overview

This project demonstrates how Business Analysis techniques can be used to understand, evaluate, and optimize business processes. The study focuses on identifying process inefficiencies, analyzing bottlenecks, measuring operational performance, and recommending improvements through process redesign and automation opportunities.

The project simulates a real-world consulting engagement where a Business Analyst evaluates an organization's order fulfillment process and proposes process optimization initiatives to improve efficiency, reduce costs, and enhance customer satisfaction.

---

# Objective

The primary objective of this project is to:

* Analyze an existing business process
* Identify operational inefficiencies and bottlenecks
* Measure process performance using key operational metrics
* Develop AS-IS and TO-BE process maps
* Recommend optimization and automation opportunities
* Create an interactive Power BI dashboard for process monitoring
* Support data-driven decision-making

---

# Business Problem

The organization is experiencing operational challenges within its order fulfillment process, including:

* Long process cycle times
* Excessive waiting and approval delays
* High process costs
* Frequent rework and process errors
* SLA violations
* Customer complaints regarding service delays

Management requires a structured process improvement initiative to identify root causes and implement solutions that enhance operational performance.

---

# Case Study Background

A mid-sized e-commerce company processes hundreds of customer orders daily through multiple departments including Sales, Operations, Inventory, Warehouse, and Logistics.

Due to increased order volume and manual activities, the company has observed:

* Delayed order processing
* Resource inefficiencies
* Increased operational costs
* Reduced customer satisfaction

This project analyzes the current workflow and proposes future-state process improvements using Business Process Mapping and Optimization methodologies.

---

# Process Mapping Methodology

The following Business Analysis methodologies were applied:

### Stakeholder Analysis

Identified key stakeholders involved in the process and documented their roles, responsibilities, and expectations.

### SIPOC Analysis

Defined process boundaries by identifying Suppliers, Inputs, Processes, Outputs, and Customers.

### AS-IS Process Mapping

Documented the current process flow to understand how work is performed today.

### Bottleneck Analysis

Analyzed activities with excessive cycle time, waiting time, and approval delays.

### Root Cause Analysis

Used techniques such as 5 Whys and Fishbone Analysis to identify underlying causes of process inefficiencies.

### Gap Analysis

Compared current-state performance against desired future-state performance.

### TO-BE Process Mapping

Designed an optimized future-state process with streamlined workflows and automation opportunities.

# Optimization Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations were proposed:

### Process Automation

Automate repetitive manual verification and approval activities.

### Workflow Simplification

Reduce unnecessary process steps and duplicate approvals.

### Inventory System Integration

Implement real-time inventory validation to reduce delays.

### Digital Approval Workflow

Replace manual approvals with automated approval mechanisms.

### Process Monitoring Dashboard

Deploy KPI dashboards for continuous process performance monitoring.

### Continuous Improvement Program

Establish regular reviews to identify future optimization opportunities.

# Key Insights

### Process Delays

Inventory validation and approval activities contributed significantly to overall cycle time.

### SLA Compliance

Several processes failed to meet established service-level targets due to excessive waiting times.

### Cost Drivers

Manual activities and rework were identified as major contributors to process costs.

### Quality Issues

Higher error rates resulted in increased rework and operational inefficiencies.

### Customer Experience

Delayed processing directly impacted customer satisfaction and complaint rates.

### Improvement Potential

Process automation and workflow redesign can significantly improve efficiency and reduce operational costs.

# Conclusion

This project demonstrates how Business Process Mapping and Process Optimization techniques can be used to improve operational performance and support business decision-making.

Through stakeholder analysis, process mapping, bottleneck identification, root cause analysis, and KPI monitoring, the organization gains visibility into process performance and opportunities for improvement.

The proposed TO-BE process design and optimization recommendations provide a roadmap for reducing cycle times, lowering operational costs, improving SLA compliance, and enhancing customer satisfaction.

This project highlights practical Business Analysis, Process Improvement, and Data Analytics skills applicable to Business Analyst, Process Analyst, Operations Analyst, and Management Consulting roles.
